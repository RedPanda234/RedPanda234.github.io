Facts

The Population of Red Panda has decreased by over 50% in the last 20 years! The relatives of the Red Panda are all extinct, meaning that the Red Panda has no living relatives. The Red Panda live in parts of China and Nepal. The main problems facing the Red Panda are deforestation and hunting. The Red Panda eats bamboo leaves and other small leaves and berries. The Red Panda is a little bigger than a house cat.

Human Impacts

Human impacts on the Red Panda are numerous but the main ones include: loss of habitat, hunting and dieases. The main causes of habitat loss can be attributed to one of the following: logging, residential and comercial development and roads.
